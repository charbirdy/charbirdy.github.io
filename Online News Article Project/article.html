What is Impressionism?

Impressionism is an art movement that came from the late 18th century in Paris, France, where it focused on capturing moments in life through colors and light. It influenced Post-Impressionism and Neo-Impressionism, whereas famous artists like Vincent Van Gogh developed their style through these.

How It Came to Life

This began in 1860s where a group of painters containing Claude Monet, Pierre-Auguste Renoir, and Camille Pissarro started to paint against strict art academic rules. Instead of following standard artist techniques, they came up with their own and started to paint moments of everyday life with visible brushstrokes. As said by Claude Monet, Impressionism is only direct sensation.

The Name's Origin

In an 1874 art exhibition, Louis Leroy Tom took a glance at a painting by Claude Monet, titled "Impression, Soleil Levant", which translates to "Impression, Sunrise". Meant as an insult, he would comment that it was an "impression" rather than a finished piece. Instead of taking it for word, artists decided to use it as the label used today, Impressionism.
